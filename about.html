About Us

The school is a distinctive Christian institution founded upon Christian principles on September 17th, 2005 at Grafton Village and was the first private school within that community then. The school started with 14 pupils, from Nursery to Prep three(3), and it kept growing from the inception up to 2008 when the first set of prep six(6) pupils took the National Primary School Examination (NPSE) and got 100% pass. Based on the outcome of the NPSE result, parents expressed the need for the introduction of High school.

The enrolment gradually increased from eight(8) to twenty seven(27) pupils by the end of that academic year. Since the commencement of the High school, tremendous progresses were made, that led to numerical growth and public recognition. However in 2011, fifteen Pupils from the school were registered under a public school to take the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE) and all of them got six subjects and above.

In 2012, both the Junior and Senior schools got provisional approval with codes to take both the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE) and the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE). The activities and outcomes of the school since 2012, have proved that Dynamic International High School (DIHS) is one of the best schools in our country.

With a well furnished computer laboratory, outstanding results in public examinations, as well as child centred educational activities that practically develop the three domains of every child who passes through the school, the need for excellence is always sought to promote standard. As an established institution, we could boast of astronomical successes over the years, as most of our former pupils of this institution are found in recognized universities and colleges in and out of Sierra Leone. However, the school met one of its greatest challenges during the Ebola crises in our country and it resulted in financial hardship in the payment of staff, our yearly rents, payment of bills and other running costs. This eventually led to the loss of the building we were occupying at 26 Banana water Drive, Off Murray Town Road, and this subsequently led to a drastic drop in the enrolment, damaged of properties, withdrawer of some staff followed by some other unexpected effects.
